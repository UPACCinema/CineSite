-          SNC: Saturday Night Coordinator

Article I: Club Purpose

 

  1. UPAC Cinema provides motion-picture entertainment to the Rensselaer community in the capacity of a non-theatrical, second-run theater. Like other Union organizations, UPAC Cinema is run entirely by students, providing the opportunity to run a fully-functional, professional-level theater and gain experience in leadership, teamwork, budgeting and interacting with industry professionals. UPAC Cinema also provides miscellaneous projection services to the Rensselaer community.

 

Article II: Ratification and Amendment

 

  1. The guidelines presented in this document shall become effective immediately upon a majority vote of all members present at the UPAC Cinema meeting in which they are presented. A quorum of not less than two-thirds of all active UPAC Cinema general members (V) and two-thirds of all officers, is required for voting to take place. Furthermore, this meeting must be announced and the new bylaws distributed no less than 2 weeks prior to the vote.

 

  1. Amendments to this document must be presented and ratified in the same manner as the document itself (II-i).

 

Article III: Officers

 

  1. All officers are required to attend all UPAC Cinema meetings and train members to succeed them in their duties. In the event that an officer or appointee is temporarily unable to fulfill their duties, they shall appoint a replacement. Officers are afforded the privilege of giving away one free ticket per film, excluding club-sponsored films. Officers may view club films for free.

 

  1. The Chair is the ultimate authority over all matters pertaining to UPAC Cinema and is responsible for the daily operations of the organization.
    1. The Chair will handle all communication with outside organizations, the Rensselear Student Union, Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute and film companies.
    2. The Chair is responsible for selecting, scheduling and booking the Pop Film Series movies as well as coordinating with the Saturday, Mid-Week and Reparatory Coordinators to schedule and book their respective film series.
    3. In dealing with film companies, the Chair handles the booking of films and acquisition of trailers and posters. The Chair reports attendance to the film companies as needed. If necessary, the Chair may appoint a film runner to handle transportation of films to and from the depots.
    4. The Chair: must ensure that all Union fiscal policies are followed; must approve bills and turn them in to the Union Administration Office for processing; manage issues related to payroll; must create and submit the UPAC Cinema budget to the Union Executive Board for processing and defend it as necessary.
    5. The Chair is responsible for obtaining the working hours of the QP’s from the Head QP in order to fill out and submit the payroll to the Union Administration Office for processing.
    6. The Chair is responsible for coordinating with the Head QP and Publicity Coordinator to ensure that supplies are kept at acceptable levels and that additional supplies are ordered as necessary.
    7. The Chair's other responsibilities include: booking the theater (DCC308), presiding over all meetings, advancing the club purpose and handling all disciplinary actions (VIII).
    8. The Chair will approve the appointments of the Head QP (III-vi) and the Team Leaders as well as appoint a Publicity Coordinator (III-x).

 

  1. The Friday Night Coordinator handles all shows in the Pop Films series. Responsibilities include ensuring the movie, vending, money and team are all run efficiently. To assist in running this series, the FNC may appoint Team Leaders (III-viii).  The FNC has authority over matters concerning Friday night movies on the night of the show.

 

  1. The Saturday Night Coordinator is responsible for handling club-sponsored movies. The SNC shall solicit clubs towards filling the semester quota of club films as defined by the budget and handle all negotiations with other club officials. In the event that more clubs apply than available nights, preference shall be given in this order: Union sponsored clubs, non-Union clubs, other Rensselaer organizations, individual students, faculty and staff. The SNC shall hold the interests of UPAC Cinema foremost in negotiations with other clubs. In the event that insufficient clubs are available to fill all Saturdays, the SNC may work with the Chair to select movies for Cinema Saturdays. The SNC has authority over all matters concerning club-sponsored and Cinema Saturday films on the night of the show.

 

  1. The Midweek and Repertory Coordinators are responsible for selecting and managing films in their respective film series with approval by the Chair. It is required that those elected for these positions be QP’s (III-vii) and that they serve as the projectionist for movies in their respective series.  The Midweek and Repertory Coordinators have authority over all matters concerning the Midweek and Repertory films on the night of the show, respectively.

 

  1. The Head QP is responsible for organizing the projectionist schedule and for submitting the working hours of the QP’s to the Chair for payroll purposes. The Head QP is also responsible for maintaining a list of active TQP’s and their contact information in the booth so that QP’s may call on them in case of emergency. The Head QP is appointed by the current QP’s and must be a QP (III-viii). The Head QP appointment is subject to approval of the Chair.

 

  1. Technician QP’s are QP’s who are extremely familiar with all of UPAC Cinema’s equipment. These highly qualified individuals are called upon when major repairs or modifications are needed. A list of active TQP’s should be maintained in the booth so that QP’s may call on them in case of emergency.

 

  1. Qualified Projectionists are the only persons authorized to use UPAC Cinema equipment. QP’s are qualified by examinations administered by the current active QP’s. QP’s may oversee PIT’s during the projection of movies and in organized training sessions. This is the only situation where an unqualified individual may operate UPAC Cinema equipment.

 

  1. Technician QP’s are QP’s who are extremely familiar with all of UPAC Cinema’s equipment. These highly qualified individuals are called upon when major repairs or modifications are needed.

 

  1. Team Leaders are responsible for ensuring that a sufficient number of members arrive to work at their appointed shows. During the night, the Team Leader is responsible for ensuring that all necessary jobs are done properly and placing the food order for the night, if any. Team Leaders may not work as PIT’s on their assigned night. The Team Leaders are appointed by the Friday Night Coordinator and approved by the Chair.  The number of Team Leaders to be assigned is at the discretion of the Friday Night Coordinator.

 

  1. The Publicity Coordinator is responsible for producing the semesterly schedules, weekly posters and movie posters as well as distributing these materials on campus in a timely manner to advertise UPAC Cinema’s films. The Publicity Coordinator may appoint a Postering Assistant at the discretion of the Chair to assist him/her. Other forms of publicity should also be routed through the Publicity Coordinator, including, but not limited to, the UPAC Cinema website.

 

Article IV: Succession

 

  1. In the event of a vacancy in one of the officer positions, the officer directly below the vacant position will assume those duties until elections can be held. Appointed officers may not move up the hierarchy in this manner. The hierarchy is as follows:
    1. Chair
    2. Friday Night Coordinator
    3. Saturday Night Coordinator
    4. Midweek Coordinator
    5. Repertory Coordinator
    6. Head Qualified Projectionist[1]
    7. Qualified Projectionists (TQP’s included)

 

Article V: Membership Guidelines

 

        i.            To be considered an active member of UPAC Cinema an individual must work at least eight shows over the course of a semester, two of which must be midnight and no more than four of which may be on security.

 

      ii.            Officers are automatically considered members and do not have to fulfill the requirements specified in Article V, Section i.

 

    iii.            Membership benefits may be granted to potential members, for their first four weeks with UPAC Cinema. If, after this time, they have not completed their membership requirement, benefits shall be suspended until they do. Membership benefits include:

    1. Entrance in the end-of-semester poster lottery
    2. Free admission to non-club movies
    3. $.25 discount on concession items while working a show
    4. Admittance to preview showings of movies

 

  1. Beyond the membership requirements it is each member’s responsibility to further the image of the club in the community. This includes, but is not limited to:
    1. Protecting our privilege to use DCC 308
    2. Promoting UPAC Cinema’s films in a positive manner
    3. Attending special UPAC Cinema events as able (i.e. UPAC Mainstage)
    4. Behaving in a courteous manner when representing UPAC Cinema

 

Article VI: Voting and Elections

 

  1. Elections for officer positions will be held once per year at the conclusion of the Fall semester and/or when a vacancy appears in an officer position. These officer positions include the Chair and the Friday Night, Saturday Night, Midweek, and Repertory coordinators.

 

  1. Nominations for officer positions will be held at a meeting at least one week prior to the election. This meeting shall be advertised for not less than two weeks prior. To be nominated, an individual's name must be submitted and seconded during the nominations, followed by a formal acceptance by the nominee.

 

  1. For both the nominations and the elections meetings, a quorum of not less than two-thirds of all active general members, including all current officers, must be present.

 

  1. The outgoing Chair will organize and run the election. In the case that the person responsible for the election is running for the position in question, responsibility for the election follows the chain of command as defined in Article IV.

 

  1. The election will proceed in order of precedence, beginning with elections for Chair. Each candidate will be permitted a short time to speak and then all candidates will leave the room during discussion and voting. The candidate who receives the most votes will be selected for the position.

 

  1. Immediately after elections, the newly elected Chair will appoint a Publicity Coordinator (III-x) and film/concessions runners as necessary. The new Friday Night Coordinator will then appoint Team Leaders (III-ix) as necessary. Team Leader appointments are subject to the approval of the Chair.

 

  1. Newly elected/appointed officers will assume their duties at the beginning of the Spring semester unless their election/appointment is the result of a vacancy as defined in Article VI, Section viii.

 

  1. For major decisions pertaining to UPAC Cinema, a vote may be held. The issue must be raised and seconded at a meeting and voting will commence at the meeting following discussion.

 

  1. In the event that an officer suddenly leaves their position or is impeached, the officer in the position directly below theirs will assume their duties (IV) until an emergency election can be held to select a replacement. This election shall be held not more than one week after the vacancy and must abide by the guidelines set forth for regular elections as specified in Article VI. Powers of these temporary officers shall be limited to vital and/or immediately relevant issues only.

 

Article VII: Bookkeeping and Records

 

  1. The Chair will maintain copies of all financial transactions and an up to date record of the club’s financial standing.

 

  1. Records shall be kept of members who work shows by show time and job. This record will be maintained by the Friday Night Coordinator. Member lists from other film series shall be submitted promptly by their respective coordinators to the FNC. The jobs which are valid for the purpose of this recording (and thus for membership requirements) are as follows:
    1. Ticket selling
    2. Ticket ripping
    3. Concession selling
    4. Security
    5. PIT

 

  1. Records shall be kept of patron attendance by show time/date and payment method. This record shall be maintained by the Chair, from submissions by the nightly coordinators.

 

  1. Records shall be kept of interactions with other clubs. These records shall include but are not limited to: the actual contract and a report by the Saturday Night Coordinator which documents the method by which the club was solicited and includes an overall summary of the interaction with the club.

 

Article VIII: Disciplinary Action

 

  1. An officer may be determined unfit for their position through a petition of three of the current officers. Once determined unfit, both sides will have the opportunity to present their cases at the following regularly scheduled meeting. The officer in question may then be impeached by a two-thirds majority vote of the current officers. This vote can be overruled if a petition signed by not less than two-thirds of all active general members is presented by the next regularly scheduled meeting.

 

  1. Disciplinary action against general members is at the discretion of the Chair.

[1] Should the Head QP position become vacant, Article V does not apply. A new Head QP must be appointed as required by Article III, Section vi.

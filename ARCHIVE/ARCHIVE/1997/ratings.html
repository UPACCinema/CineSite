What the MPAA Movie Ratings Mean

The Motion Picture Code and Rating Administration began applying the following designations November 1, 1968. All films released after the rating system began carry a letter designation.

G

General Audiences. All ages admitted.

PG

Parental Guidance suggested. Some material may be unsuitable for children.

PG-13

Parents are stringly cautioned to give special guidance for attendance of children under 13. Some material may not be appropriate for young children.

R

Restricted - People under 17 years of age require an accompanying parent or guardian.

NC-17

No one under 17 years of age admitted to this film.

X

This rating symbol is no longer used to classify films (no one under 17 admitted), and may not be identified with NC-17 without the specific authorization of the Classification and Rating Administration.

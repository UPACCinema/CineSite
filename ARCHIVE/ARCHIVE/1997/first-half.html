The Fifth Element

Friday, August 29
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Pop Film Series
PG-13 -- 127 Minutes
Starring : Milla Jovovich, Gary Oldman and Bruce Willis

In the distant future, all life on the Earth is about to come to an end. Evil is poised ready to destroy the planet. The only thing that can stop Evil is the Fifth Element, a weapon of immense power. To activate the Fifth Element, taxi driver Korben Dallas must find the necessary key stones before the minions of Evil can stop him.

The Devil's Own

Saturday, August 30
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Saturday Film Series
R -- 111 Minutes
Starring : Margaret Colin, Harrison Ford and Brad Pitt

When Irish Republican Army assassin Frankie Maguire flees to the United States to buy weapons, his host, an IRA sympathizing judge in New York City places him as Rory Devaney in the good-natured home of police officer Tom O'Meara. O'Meara does not suspect anything about his guest until Frankie's past catches up with him and threatens to disrupt the O'Meara household.

Angels and Insects

Wednesday, September 3
7pm and 10pm

Repertory Film Series
R -- 117 Minutes
Starring : Patsy Kensit, Chris Larkin and Mark Rylance

In 1860's England, William Adamson returns from 10 years of study in South America with nothing -- a shipwreck washed away his possessions. Offered a residence at the estate of the Reverend Harold Alabaster in return for his intellectual guidance, Adamson finds himself enamored of the Alabaster's daughter, Eugenia. Her love is uncertain and a dark past clouds any possibility of marriage.

Liar - Liar

Friday, September 5
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Pop Film Series
PG -- 86 Minutes
Starring : Jim Carrey, Justin Cooper and Maura Tierney

Speaking your mind is never a problem unless you must always tell the truth. Saddened by his father missing his birthday party, Max Reid makes a wish that his father, a fast talking lawyer cannot tell a lie for 24 hours. When the wish comes true, Fletcher Reid finds that his big mouth is a big liability and his inability to lie is ruining his career.

Howard Stern's Private Parts

Saturday, September 6
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Saturday Film Series
R -- 109 Minutes
Starring : Mary McCormack, Robin Quivers and Howard Stern

Based on Howard Stern's autobiography, this film chronicles his life. From his early interest in radio through his days in college and his first jobs, we see how his on air persona developed and what forces acted to make Howard Stern one of the most popular radio DJs of all time.

Murder at 1600

Wednesday, September 10
7pm and 10pm

Midweek Film Series
R -- 107 Minutes
Starring : Alan Alda, Diane Lane and Wesley Snipes

Homicide detective Harlan Regis is called in to investigate the murder of a female staff member found dead in the White House. As his investigation proceeds, he finds that the Secret Service is constantly one step ahead of him. Fearing a cover up, he convinces agent Nina Chance to help him, but begins to get suspicious of her behavior as well. His only hope is to uncover the conspiracy before it is too late.

The Saint

Friday, September 12
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Pop Film Series
PG-13 -- 116 Minutes
Starring : Val Kilmer, Rade Serbedzija and Elisabeth Shue

Simon Templar, the Saint, is a master thief. He has no limits on who he steals from or what he will take, so long as the money is good. When he tries to steal a formula for cold fusion from Dr. Emma Russel, he finds love is a greater power than money. As the person who hired him comes after Dr. Russel, the Saint finds himself protecting her at all costs.

Volcano

Saturday, September 13
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Saturday Film Series
PG-13 -- 102 Minutes
Starring : Anne Heche, Gaby Hoffman and Tommy Lee Jones

Mike Roark is a supervisor for the Los Angeles' Office of Emergency Management. While on vacation, a small earthquake hits L.A. causing some suspicious deaths. Seismologist Dr. Amy Barnes has a theory, but nobody is willing to believe her -- until lava erupts all over Wilshire Boulevard. Roark must then race against time to stop the lava before it destroys all of downtown Los Angeles.

Army Of Darkness

Wednesday, September 17
7pm and 10pm

Repertory Film Series
R -- 85 Minutes
Starring : Ian Abercrombie, Bruce Campbell and Embeth Davidtz

Name's Ash. Housewares. Unfortunately, for S-Mart customers, he won't be much help since he's trapped in the 13th century. Mistaken for a spy of a rival kingdom, Ash proves his worth in the Pit and is recruited to help recover the Necronomicon. It's a book powerful enough to return him to his own time if he uses it properly. But a misspoken phrase releases the Army of Darkness and Ash must clean up the mess before he can return home.

Stand And Deliver

Thursday, September 18
TBA

Lecture Film Series
PG -- ?? Minutes
Starring : Mark Eliot, Estelle Harris and Edward James Olmos

Jamie Escalante is a math teacher in a high school in a Hispanic neighborhood. Believing that his students have untapped potential, he uses any technique he can think of to entice his students to want to do better. Over time, the techniques pay off as he turns gang members into some of the top calculus and algebra students in the nation.

Batman And Robin

Friday, September 19
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Pop Film Series
PG-13 -- 130 Minutes
Starring : George Clooney, Arnold Schwarzenegger and Alicia Silverstone

The caped crusader is back! This time he must stop the evil Mr. Freeze before he puts all of Gotham on ice. Batman also has to stop the plans of the seductive Poison Ivy, who is bent on destroying the dynamic duo. With the help of Batgirl, the trio is able to thaw out Mr. Freeze, put Poison Ivy's plans on ice and save the citizens of Gotham from certain doom.

Shine

Saturday, September 20
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Saturday Film Series
PG-13 -- 105 Minutes
Starring : Lynn Redgrave, Geoffrey Rush and Noah Taylor

Australian pianist David Helfgott faced many demons. As a child, he displayed a marked virtuosity in piano playing and was destined for a career as a concert musician. However, mistreatment by his domineering father stifles his growth at every turn. Twenty years after being institutionalized, his release brings new hope and through strange twists of fate, he returns to performing in concerts.

Inventing The Abbots

Wednesday, September 24
7pm and 10pm

Midweek Film Series
R -- 110 Minutes
Starring : Kathy Baker, Will Patton and Joaquin Phoenix

In 1957 Illinois, Doug & Jacey Holt and Alice, Elanor and Pamela Abbott are neighbors. Separated by class, but joined by a common history -- their fathers were business partners, the Holt brothers find themselves in competition for the affections of the Abbott sisters. However, their overprotective father, Lloyd Abbott, does not approve of any liaisons with a lower class, leading to conflict between both families.

Con Air

Friday, September 26
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Pop Film Series
R -- 115 Minutes
Starring : Nicholas Cage, John Cusack and John Malkovich

An airplane transporting convicts to a Louisiana prison is hijacked by the prisoners. Headed to Las Vegas, the prisoners plan to meet with another plane and leave the country. The only thing to stop them is a passenger on the plane, Cameron Poe, a former U.S. Army officer who is being paroled after 8 years in prison. With the help of U.S. Marshal Vince Larkin on the ground, he must stop the prisoners in the air before their escape is successful.

Sekena

Saturday, September 27
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers
PG -- 127 Minutes
Starring : Jennifer Lopez, Edward James Olmos and Jon Seda

Selena is the story of Tejano singer Selena Quintanilla Perez and her rise to fame. It is also the story of how her family, especially her father Abraham, managed to shape her life and helped Selena realize her dreams. These dreams carry her to new heights of popularity as a singer about to go mainstream, until one day when her path crosses with that of an obsessed fan.

Crash

Wednesday, October 1
7pm and 10pm

Repertory Film Series
NC-17 -- 100 Minutes
Starring : Rosanna Arquette, Holly Hunter and James Spader

James Ballard and his wife Catherine are in an open marriage. Their lives change drastically when James is responsible for a car crash. Befriending the survivor, Dr. Helen Remington, James is initiated into a society of people who are fascinated with the sexual rush of automobile accidents. Passions reach new levels as the group explores physical and sexual boundaries of this auto-eroticism.

Men In Black

Friday, October 3
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Pop Film Series
PG-13 -- 98 Minutes
Starring : Linda Fiorentino, Tommy Lee Jones and Will Smith

Aliens have been living on the Earth for the last 40 years. The Men In Black, a super secret organization, is responsible for keeping track of all of them. When veteran Agent K needs a new partner, he recruits James Edwards, a New York City cop. Together, Agent K and Agent J must stop an intergalactic assassin who¹s plans could spell the end of the Earth.

The Lost World

Saturday, October 4
7pm, 9:30pm and 12am

Saturday Film Series
PG-13 -- 134 Minutes
Starring : Jeff Goldblum, Julianne Moore and Pete Postlethwaite

Isla Sorna - home to InGen Corporation¹s secret breeding ground for the dinosaurs of Jurassic Park. Ian Malcolm leads a group of researchers studying the island, left alone since the disaster four years earlier. Once there, they must stop a group of InGen researchers from capturing the dinosaurs for a new park and prevent the disaster of Jurassic Park from happening all over again.

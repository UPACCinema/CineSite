UPAC Cinema

By-Laws

 

Revision History:

Feb2004        Written            Gavin Olson, Emilio Perez

 

Abbreviations:

The following abbreviations are used in this document:

-         FNC: Friday Night Coordinator

-         SNC: Saturday Night Coordinator

-         QP: Qualified Projectionist

-         PIT: Projectionist in Training

-         DCC: Darrin Communications Center

 

Article I: Club Purpose

 

  1. As a non-theatrical organization, UPAC Cinema provides motion-picture entertainment to the Rensselaer community in the capacity of a second-run theater. Like other Union organizations, UPAC Cinema is run entirely by students, providing the opportunity to run a fully-functional, professional-level theater and gain experience in leadership, teamwork, budgeting and interacting with industry professionals. UPAC Cinema also provides miscellaneous projection services to the Rensselaer community.

 

Article II: Ratification and Amendment

 

  1. The guidelines presented in this document shall become effective immediately upon a majority vote of all members present at the UPAC Cinema meeting in which they are presented. A quorum of not less than 12 active UPAC Cinema members, 6 of which must be general members (V), is required for voting to take place. Furthermore, this meeting must be announced and the new bylaws distributed no less than 2 weeks prior to the vote.

 

  1. Amendments to this document must be presented and ratified in the same manner as the document itself (II-i).

 

Article III: Policies

 

  1. DCC policies prohibit smoking and the possession or consumption of alcohol in the building. It is the policy of UPAC Cinema to refer all matters involving alcohol to the office of Public Safety. In other matters, such as smoking or unruly behavior, the offending patrons will be removed by the coordinator on duty.

 

  1. UPAC Cinema's use of DCC308 is a privilege, not a right. As such, UPAC Cinema should not engage in any activity that might endanger that privilege. This includes keeping the room and surrounding areas (including the projection booth) clean and presentable.

 

  1. DCC308 must be reserved for all UPAC Cinema movie events. UPAC Cinema will attempt to negotiate reasonably with any organization wishing to use the room during normal movie times.

 

  1. Only UPAC Cinema certified QP’s (IV-vii) are permitted to use UPAC Cinema equipment. A PIT may operate equipment only under the supervision of a QP.

 

Article IV: Officers

 

  1. The Chair is the ultimate authority over all matters pertaining to UPAC Cinema and is responsible for the daily operations of the organization.
    1. The Chair will handle all communication with outside organizations, the Union, Rensselaer and film companies.
    2. The Chair is responsible for selecting and scheduling the Pop Film Series movies.
    3. In dealing with film companies, the Chair handles the booking of films, trailers and posters. The Chair reports attendance to the film companies as needed. If necessary, the Chair may appoint a film runner to handle transportation of films to and from the depots.
    4. The Chair must ensure that all Union fiscal policies are followed. He/She must approve bills and turn them in to the Union Administration Office for processing. The Chair also manages issues related to payroll. The Chair must also create and submit the UPAC Cinema budget to the Union Executive Board for processing and defend it as necessary.
    5. The Chair is responsible for ensuring that supplies are kept at acceptable levels and that additional supplies are ordered as necessary.
    6. The Chair's other responsibilities include: booking the theater (DCC308), presiding over all meetings, advancing the club purpose and handling all disciplinary actions (VIII).
    7. The Chair will appoint the Head QP (IV-v).

 

  1. The Friday Night Coordinator handles all shows in the Pop Films series. Responsibilities include all those not expressly handled by the Chair. To assist in running this series, the FNC may appoint Team Leaders (IV-viii).  The FNC has authority over matters concerning Friday night movies on the night of the show.

 

  1. The Saturday Night Coordinator is responsible for handling club-sponsored movies. The SNC shall solicit clubs towards filling the semester quota of club films as defined by the budget and handle all negotiations with other club officials. In the event that more clubs apply than available nights, preference shall be given in this order: Union sponsored clubs, non-Union clubs, other Rensselaer organizations, individual students, faculty and staff. The SNC shall hold the interests of UPAC Cinema foremost in negotiations with other clubs. In the event that insufficient clubs are available to fill all Saturdays, the SNC may work with the Chair to select movies for Cinema Saturdays. The SNC has authority over all matters concerning club-sponsored and Cinema Saturday films on the night of the show.

 

  1. The Midweek and Repertory Coordinators are responsible for selecting and managing films in their respective film series with approval by the Chair. It is required that those elected for these positions be QP’s (IV-vii).  The Midweek and Repertory Coordinators have authority over all matters concerning the Midweek and Repertory films on the night of the show, respectively.

 

  1. The Head QP is responsible for organizing the projectionist schedule and is also responsible for managing the projectionists’ payroll.

 

  1. Technician QP’s are QP’s who are extremely familiar with all of UPAC Cinema’s equipment. These highly qualified individuals are called upon when major repairs or modifications are needed. A list of active TQP’s should be maintained in the booth so that QP’s may call on them in case of emergency.

 

  1. Qualified Projectionists are the only persons authorized to use UPAC Cinema equipment. QP’s are qualified by examinations administered by the current active QP’s. QP’s may oversee PIT’s during the projection of movies and in organized training sessions. This is the only situation where an unqualified individual may operate UPAC Cinema equipment.

 

  1. Team Leaders are responsible for ensuring that a sufficient number of members arrive to work at their appointed shows. During the night, the Team Leader is responsible for ensuring that all necessary jobs are done properly and placing the food order for the night, if any. The Team Leaders are appointed by the Friday Night Coordinator and approved by the Chair.  The number of Team Leaders to be assigned is at the discretion of the Friday Night Coordinator.

 

  1. The Publicity Coordinator is responsible for producing the semesterly schedules, weekly posters and movie posters as well as distributing these materials on campus in a timely manner to advertise UPAC Cinema’s films. The Publicity Coordinator may appoint a Postering Assistant at the discretion of the Chair to assist him/her. Other forms of publicity should also be routed through the Publicity Coordinator.

 

  1. In the event of a vacancy in one of the officer positions, the officer directly below the vacant position will assume those duties until elections can be held. Appointed officers may not move up the hierarchy in this manner. The hierarchy is as follows:
    1. Chair
    2. Friday Night Coordinator
    3. Saturday Night Coordinator
    4. Midweek Coordinator
    5. Repertory Coordinator
    6. Head Qualified Projectionist
    7. Qualified Projectionists (TQP’s included)

 

  1. All officers are required to attend all UPAC Cinema meetings and train members to succeed them in their duties. In the event that an officer or appointee is temporarily unable to fulfill their duties, they shall appoint a replacement. Officers are afforded the privilege of giving away one free ticket per film, excluding club-sponsored films. Officers may view club films for free.

 

Article V: Membership Guidelines

 

        i.            To be considered an active member of UPAC Cinema an individual must work at least eight shows over the course of a semester, two of which must be midnight and no more than four of which may be on security.

 

      ii.            Officers are automatically considered members and do not have to fulfill the requirements in the above section.

 

    iii.            Membership benefits may be granted to potential members, for their first four weeks with UPAC Cinema. If, after this time, they have not completed their membership requirement, benefits shall be suspended until they do. Membership benefits include:

    1. Entrance in the end-of-semester poster lottery
    2. Free admission to non-club movies
    3. $.25 discount on concession items while working a show
    4. Admittance to preview showings of movies

 


  1. Beyond the membership requirements it is each member’s responsibility to further the image of the club in the community. This includes, but is not limited to:
    1. Promoting UPAC Cinema’s films in a positive manner
    2. Attending non-regularly scheduled UPAC Cinema events as able (i.e. UPAC Mainstage)
    3. Behaving in a courteous manner when representing UPAC Cinema

 

Article VI: Voting and Elections

 

  1. Elections for officer positions will be held once per year at the conclusion of the Fall semester and when a vacancy appears in an officer position. These officer positions include the Chair and the Friday Night, Saturday Night, Midweek and Repertory coordinators.

 

  1. Nominations for officer positions will be held at a meeting one week prior to the election. This meeting shall be advertised for not less than two weeks prior. To be nominated, an individual's name must be submitted and seconded during the nominations followed by a formal acceptance by the nominee.

 

  1. For both the nominations meeting and the election meeting, a quorum of not less than 8 people, including at least 4 general members, must be present.

 

  1. The outgoing Chair will organize and run the election. In the case that the person responsible for the election is running for the position in question, responsibility for the election follows the chain of command (IV-x).

 

  1. The election will proceed in order of precedence, beginning with elections for Chair. Each candidate will be permitted a short time to speak and then all candidates will leave the room during discussion and voting. The candidate who receives the most votes will be selected for the position.

 

  1. Immediately after elections, the newly elected Chair will appoint a Publicity Coordinator (IV-ix) and film/concessions runners as necessary. The new Friday Night Coordinator will then appoint Team Leaders (IV-viii) as necessary. Team Leader appointments are subject to the approval of the Chair.

 

  1. For major decisions pertaining to UPAC Cinema, a vote may be held. The issue must be raised and seconded at a meeting and voting will commence at the meeting following discussion.

 

  1. In the event that an officer suddenly leaves their position, the officer in the position directly below theirs will assume their duties (IV-x) until an emergency election can be held to select a replacement. This election shall be held not more than one week after the vacancy.

 

Article VII: Bookkeeping and Records

 

  1. The Chair will maintain copies of all financial transactions and an up to date record of the club’s financial standing.

 

  1. Records shall be kept of members who work shows by show time and job. This record will be maintained by the Friday Night Coordinator. Member lists from other film series shall be submitted promptly by their respective coordinators to the FNC. The jobs which are valid for the purpose of this recording (and thus for membership requirements) are as follows:
    1. Ticket selling
    2. Ticket ripping
    3. Concession selling
    4. Security
    5. PIT

 

  1. Records shall be kept of patron attendance by show time/date and payment method. This record shall be maintained by the Chair, from submissions by the nightly coordinators.

 

  1. Records shall be kept of interactions with other clubs. These records shall include but are not limited to: the actual contract and a report by the Saturday Night Coordinator which documents the method by which the club was solicited and includes an overall summary of the interaction with the club.

 

Article VIII: Disciplinary Action

 

  1. An officer may be determined unfit for their position through a petition of three of the current officers. Once determined unfit, an officer may be impeached by a majority vote of active members attending a regular meeting. This meeting must be advertised no less than two weeks in advance and be attended by a quorum of at least eight general members.

 

  1. Disciplinary action against general members is at the discretion of the Chair.
